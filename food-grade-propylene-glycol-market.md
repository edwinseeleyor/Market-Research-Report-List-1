<p><p>The food-grade propylene glycol market comprises three main types: Mono Propylene Glycol (MPG), Dipropylene Glycol (DPG), and Tripropylene Glycol (TPG). MPG is commonly utilized as a food additive and solvent due to its low toxicity and high stability. DPG serves as a humectant and emulsifier, enhancing product texture and moisture retention, while TPG, with its higher molecular weight, is used in specialty applications, including flavorings and fragrances, providing unique functionality in food processing and preservation.</p></p>
